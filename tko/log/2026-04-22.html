Tabletop campaigns

2026-04-22 (It's always sunny in Omu)

We retcon the weather to say that it's sunny. We decide to start the day heading toward the water temple. As we get to the water we're able to see that it is a couple feet deep with a gentle current. Thankfully the buildings are blocking most of the current. We try to stealth but get middling successes and seem to just enjoy the sun and water. Jack notices four crocodiles in the muddy banks near the water temple. We see Eblis (birds) carved in the columns around the water temple. We know it's a trickster god, Papazotl. Jack talks to the crocodiles using "Speak with Animals" and offers food for safe passage. We enter the temple and see moss clinging to the stonework throughout this hall. The mosaic floor depicts a tall bird using its long pointy beak to pin a monstrous frog-like creature with tentacles sprouting from its shoulders. Set into the far wall is a set of heavy stone doors.

Orvex reads the Omuan on the mosaic floor. "A wily Eblis stepped from his reed hut. He didn't like the Omuans, but without them he'd have no one to play his tricks on. The Eblis sent a Marsh Frog to reason with Ubtao, but the frog was angry and decided to wrestle the god instead. This amused Ubtao, so he gave the frog tentacles to make it stronger. When Kubazan, the frog he met returned to Papazotl, the Eblis, he chased Papazotl into the swamp with his new tentacles."

Jack tries to open the doors on the back wall and can't get them open. Yormash then tries and fails as well. Fitz determines the doors aren't locked, we just need more force to open the door. We all work together and succeed in opening the doors. We see an empty pedestal standing in the middle of this chamber. Six statues face it from the alcoves in the wall. They depict bare-chested humans with the heads of different animals. From left to right, the heads resemble those of a lizard, a jaguar, a lobster, a toucan, a bat, and a frog.

Yormash and Fitz start investigating and find a riddle on a pedestal. "Comes with sunshine / Leaves with night / Hides in darkness / Does not bite / Always joined to it's caster / Never strays from it's master". We determine the solution is "shadow". We try closing the door and putting a Nut! light or torch behind each statue to get the statue shadows aligned on the pedestal. As we approach the statues, we make perception checks. Only Steve sees a shadow that shouldn't be there. It's a two foot by three foot square and we all go look at it. Fitz goes in and crawls about 10 feet to a new room. He sees a strange grid in the stone wall of the cell.

We figure out that this is a cipher and use the letters from the riddle in the cipher to determine what the message is. The cipher says "cover eyes". We all then head to the statues and each cover the statues eyes. The puzzle cube comes from the hatch on the pedestal. We have Orvex grab the cube before we take our hands off the statues eyes. As we pull our hands away from the eyes, the hatch closes. We now have four cubes in our possessions through five different shrines. As we head out of the temple it's about lunchtime. The initial crocodiles must have told their friend as there's a giant crocodile next to the other crocodiles. Jack once again cast speak with animals and convinces him that we will give it rations if they will let us pass safely. We all pass by giving rations just like last time.

As we head south, we determine that we are going to go into the city like structure since the water isn't quite as high as we thought it was. We tie ropes around ourselves and get to the wall surrounding the circular city area. We then go East and go into the circled area. As we enter, we see hundreds of bats flying overhead. Behind a 15-foot high circular wall, you see crumbling arcades, vine choked statues, empty plazas, and buildings overgrown with banyan roots. Streets that aren't flooded are choked with rubble. Jack decides to try fishing again and catches a fish.

As we exit the water area, we see three snakes in our path. Behind those snakes, we see an intentionally made tunnel with cart paths inside tunnel. Jack tries to cast animal friendship on the snakes and due to the snakes not being animals, they do not become his friend. They then transform into Yuan-ti. They feel threatened and one goes to ring a gong. We fight and we kill them and we throw them over the wall and let the current take them down river.

Rewards and experience.

  • 1500xp for solving the temple and fighting nasty snakes (21655xp total)
