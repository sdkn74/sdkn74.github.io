As we approach the maze under the amphitheater, we aren't stealthy in six dinos flank us. During the battle Jack casts Fireball on everyone. Yormash went down, but is spared at the end. As we start getting into the maze, we find a case with an Electrum platter (25gp), an Oumuan death mask painted gold, (250gp), and a Helm of Telepathy. No one seemed to really want the Helm of Telepathy, so Yormash took it. We determine that the King of Feathers is home for the day, so we hunker down to rest early.

The next day is sunny, and the King of Feathers has left, so we head east to explore the city more. We find the Shrine of Wongo and see there are five entrances. As we walk in through the center corridor, we hear monkeys in our heads. We head into the shrine, and Orvex reads an inscription that says "It's better to be Wongo's friend than his enemy". Orvax reads another inscription, "Wongo's friends know where to pour the water." As we get to the shrine's main room we see a giant statue of a Su-Monster. They're splayed with hands and feet cupped. On the walls behind, a sculpt relief shows a monkey-like creature tearing into a giant serpent. Cuneiform inscriptions are carved above and below the relief, four masks of painted stone protrude from the walls. They depict the heads of a lion, a zebra, a boar, and a vulture. Yormash and Steve go down the other four corridors and look through masks that are mounted on the walls. Yormash fails the saving throw and turns into a lion. Steve fails the second saving throw and turns into a vulture. As we look through these masks, we see that looking through the lion mask, the right hand has a beam of blue light on it. If we look through the boar mask, we see that the left foot has the beam of blue light on it. We look through the zebra mask and see that the right foot is illuminated by the blue light, and looking through the vulture mask, we see that the left hand is illuminated by the blue light. There's a riddle on the wall as well, above each mask we see the following. The lion says, "I ate one of the boar's friends." The boar says, "The vulture is lucky to be alive." The zebra says, "My only friend starved to death." And the vulture says, "One of the others have no friends." Jack and Fitz determine that we need to pour water on the left hand (vulture mask). Jack hears Wongo say in his head, "You must curse your friends or survive a battle with my children." Four Su-Monsters appear and we fight them. Yormash and Steve have been returned to their original forms. Steve picked up the cube after the fight and we noticed that the gates were still closed. We all decide to work together to lift the gate and are able to get through. We start going north. As we get to the cliff, your mash sees a vine-covered obelisk. It seems like this is the tomb entrance.
