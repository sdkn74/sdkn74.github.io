After the fight with the gargoyles, Fitz checks their bodies and sees that they're made of stone, so they're not carrying anything. We take our long rest. All three watches were uneventful. The weather in the morning is giant lightning storms. We head South outside the circle of the city and Jack sees a building that looks like a temple. We head towards a temple and we see a pair of needle-like columns marking the entrance to a walled ruin. In the courtyard beyond, Banyan roots envelop a squat building. The stone doors that seal the entrance are carved with images of snarling bears with long, sharp claws. Orvex notices that the bears are Zorbos and calls out that this was the temple of Obo'laka. He then reads the inscription on the wall, "Obalaka teaches us to tread wearily and stay in the light." As we enter the temple we see the walls of the Dark Corridor are covered with painted reliefs showing Omuan people hunting, harvesting fruit, planting crops, crafting weapons, feasting, fighting, weaving, and skimming animals. Eight empty torch brackets protrude from the walls under the reliefs.

Fitz uses Mage Hand to place torches in slots along the hallway. Fitz sees a secret door, calls it out. We determine the door is locked so Steve tries to ritual cast a Detect Magic on it. While he's doing that Fitz and Yormash finish putting torches in place. We notice a door at the end of the hall after placing all the torches. After the spell the door is not magical. Steve tries to use a Knock scroll on the secret door, but the door is still locked. It did sound like a latch was unlocked, though. Jack tries to break it down, but fails. We decide to go into the other room through the door at the end of the hall. As we enter we see a glorious Sun is carved on the ceiling directly above a pool of still water. The ceiling reflected in the pool doesn't show a Sun carving, but rather a Moon carving. Fitz brings a torch to water, but doesn't even see his reflection.

Fitz decides to put his arm in the water and doesn't feel water around his hand, just a ring around his arm. As Yormash hears this he starts feeling reckless and sticks his head through. He sees a room very similar to the Sun room, but it has a Moon on the ceiling. He pulls his head back and tells everyone about the other room. Jack decides to dive in unprompted. Yormash tries to flip himself through to prevent the gravity shift from messing him up. Steve pencil dives in, and Fitz just walks in casually. As we're investigating this room, we see another secret door. Fitz rolls a natural 20 on the investigation and sees that a key on the wall in the sculptures. He pushes on the key and the secret door opens. We pull out Nut! lights and use our torches down the secret passage. As we go through to the end we open up the other secret door. We go back to the Moon room and go through the unhidden door in the Moon room.

We see eight sconces with magical torches lit. Obo'laka fighting I'jin on the tile floor. A statue of Red Wizard holding a stone puzzle cube is on the pedestal. We're trying to figure out the puzzle and jack thinks it's related to getting the cube when you're on the Moon side. Jack goes through the Moon room portal back to the Sun side and goes through the secret passage for the puzzle cube. He touches the statue and nothing happens. Yormash gives a magical torch to Jack just in case. Fitz flashes geniuses Jack to help him as he picks up the cube. Jack feels an effect of the cube and we roll initiative, basically going into stop time. Jack feeling the effect of the cube puts the cube back. We remember that the hallway had 8 places for torches and the current room has 8 torches. We put the magical torches into the hallway sconces that we saw earlier. We see that the puzzle cube unpetrifies and we grab it.

Yormash breaks the head off the statue in case it unpetrifies. We take the head of the statue to Orvex to see if he recognizes the person. As we leave the temple, we find four dead Zorbo's that Artus and Dragonbait killed while we were in the temple. We show the head of the Red Wizard to Orvex and he mentions that the person usually carried items and could have possible goodies. We decide against burning a greater restoration use for possible goodies and keep moving to the next temple. An arrow flies towards Jack and we are left on a cliffhanger.
