2026-03-04 (Jack's busting Nuts! for rabbits)

We wake up with thunder rumbling in the distance. We use the Alchemy Jug to create water and then we try to stealth outside for a bit. The noise from last night was a distance from us from up North. Jack tries to stealth ahead but gets freaked out by lightning and thunder and falls in the mud. We get to the clearing where we fought the wasps and Fitz sees giant three-toed prints in the mud. Fitz thinks it's a T-Rex, which we all think back and realize is the King of Feathers. Orvex thought that was just a myth and is surprised by this information. As we go into a building with tarps over a bazaar, we see an area where dust was disturbed. We find an empty bone vial and Fitz realizes it's an Aarakocra bone. We have to roll a wisdom save and end up in a fight with six creatures hanging from their tails above us (Su-monsters).

After the fight, Fitz finds a potion of vitality. At the back of the building, there's a dense forest so we go back to the stone walkway trying to stealth up towards the north. Before we get too far Fitz notices a shrine, a bit southeast, so we head to that instead. It is I'Jin's shrine. We ask Orvex about I'Jin and we hear that she's an Almiraj, a unicorn rabbit. We see a burrow of Almiraj in front of the shrine and Jack cracks open a Nut! light to drop in front of the burrow hole and an Almiraj quickly grabs it and runs back inside the burrow. We head to the shrine and see sculptural reliefs decorate the doors of the shrine the carvings show a horned rabbit charging a small feral looking bear with sharp claws. There is also a message in old Omuan that Orvex translates the message and tells us that I'Jin teaches us to take the path least expected.

We find a 10 foot wide, 20 foot long hall with 32 tiles. Since the translation talks about going down the path least expected Jack walks in a straight line on the left side of the hall and gets by with no issue. We then decide to all take different columns of tiles and just walk a straight line and make it through. After we get past the hall, we come across what seems to be a maze. We go through the maze choosing left and right directions with some of us falling into traps and dodging them and others having almost no issues. At one point Yormash notices a set of footprints heading through the maze but the opposite direction (like they're leaving). Once we get through the maze, we see a pedestal with no puzzle cube on it. We investigate around and assume that the puzzle cube has been taken already and so we exit the shrine.

Yormash decides to fly up after exiting the shrine to see if he can't see more shrines. He doesn't see any since he rolled back poorly. But he does see a tree crossing the river downstream and an interesting building which has been marked on our map. I think about the fact that Fitz is pretty smart so Yormash swoops down to pick up Fitz. Fitz is then carried into the air and confirms that the interesting building is indeed a shrine. Fits also sees a little compound and there seems to be an overturn wagon to the northwest.

Once we get down, we walk towards it. We see an overturned wagon rides on the street ahead, rotting flower petals litter the ground around it, and a bee's nest (regular bees) in its broken wheels. A freshly cut of garland of jungle flowers hangs from the wagon's top. Fitz knows that everything here are offerings. Jack and Steve notice stone discs around and under the wagon. Yormash places a rock that looks like a diamond on the disc and a Chwinga takes it in waves. Yormash gently tosses another rock that looks like a diamond far in front of the Chwinga offering it to it. The Chwinga notices Steve and jumps into his beard and makes himself at home. We continue north after this wagon endeavor and we see a ruined amphitheater. There are animal statues that are lining the stands of the amphitheater. We notice five foot long clawed footprints and giant vibrant feathers. Yormash and Jack take some of the feathers into their pack. As we're looking around, we also see giant piles of dung. As we're discussing leaving and getting away from what we assume is the resting place of the King of Feathers, Yormash notices little piles of poop piling up on his shoulder. After looking around and not seeing the king of feathers, we believe it is Donny, the fairy dragon from before. After calling out for Donny we suddenly hear, "No, my name is Marky Mark." Jack sees him after disguising himself as Donny and then casts animal friendship. We speak to Marky Mark for a while and he lets us know about the King of Feather's hunting patterns evil gargoyles that are patrolling the cliffs around the city. He tells us about passages and a maze that is under the amphitheater. And that people go in, but they never come back out. We get intrigued by this, so we decide to go in and check out what's going on.

Rewards and experience.
