Tabletop campaigns

2026-06-22 (Why don't dead things stay dead?)

We see vampires standing intentionally in the shade under some trees. Jack does notice that these are vampires and asks Sketch if he's okay. He also notices the boarded up windows on the church from the inside. Sketch lets Jack know that his blood technique doesn't work on the vampires. Sketch stops backing up and recognizes the vampire tossing the orb up and down, Ciran. Woody gives us all Bardic Inspiration and casts Invisibility on us all. He then uses Mage Hand to grab the orb from Ciran and smash it on the ground. He also gets the orb in the tree before getting noticed. There's one orb remaining on the tree as we fight the vampires.

After killing all the vampires, Sketch takes the iron pendant within an engraved heart off Ciran. Woody touches the last orb and sees the little boy running in a park. He thinks it's a girl believing it's Andi. Jack hears voices in the chapel wondering if we were there to help. Jack yells out that we're not vampires. They definitely don't believe us. Sketch explains the scenario and what just went down. Someone takes a look at us through a Judas window (small window in a door, often in castles). Someone calls out "We're not inviting you in" and opens the door to the chapel. Jack mentions that he has no teeth and Sketch just walks inside breaking one of the vampires taboos. Sketch proves he's not a vampire by holding silver, eating garlic, all the normal things. Brother Branch is wearing nicer clothing and seems to be a bit higher rank than the middle aged woman who is Sister Lilac. They broke furniture to blockade the doors on windows and they've been sleeping there for a while and are low on food. Brother Branch asks if there are orbs left and Woody passes the last one over. Brother Branch mentions that the orbs are new souls, not quite ready for this world. They could be recent dead or younger souls. Woody breaks down crying that he didn't know and thought they were evil things the vampires were using. Woody grovels at the feet asking if Andi is in an orb and Brother Branch says yes. He shows Woody a light blue orb. Woody touches it and is flooded with memories from Andi. Woody communicates with the orb and gets memory impressions back of how Andi died.

Tenebracanthos asks about Tuathal and Brother Branch talks about meeting someone a couple months ago and how they went to Heart's Grove. He mentions that bight is all around the Heart's Grove. He also mentions that the vampires talking seem to be spawn of Crimson Abbot and they are looking for the Grove. Brother Branch continues on saying that there are blight spores affecting everyone and it's gotten worse over the past week. For healing he gives us a potion of greater healing and sap from a Witchwood tree. We ask if anyone has explored in there and he mentions another brother went into the woods, Brother Willow. He unfortunately has not been heard from in some time. We head to the blighted area and find it after a couple hours.

As we approach the entrance to the blight we see the trees have black rot growing down them. Light greenish spores are in the air. This is Heart's Blight. The effects are cumulative. It affects all creatures and it is classified as poison. There are no resistances to it. As we get closer we find a dead animal infested with spores. There's a huge effigy of an elven-like creature made from wood. There's a rotting stag at the base. The effigy looks like Tuathal. Tenebracanthos inspects it and doesn't get anything after looking at it. He sets it on fire. He hears a monotone voice from the stag. "The rot is inevitable. Not even the gods can stop the blighted." Jack throws the corpse into the fire after Tenebracanthos smashes its head. Jack creates a tiny hut for us to sleep in and we take a long rest.

As we enter the blighted wood, it takes us a while as the undergrowth is rotted. We find ourselves in an area with rotting black and green pine needles, rotting ribs seemingly come up from the needles and blue fungus covers a bear skull. Jack sees bear traps and points it out, throws the scimitar at the bear skull, and spores fly out. We wait a second to let the spores die down again. Tenebracanthos uses a bunch of crows to fly (crows, honestly). Woody just flies. Sketch and Jack navigate through the woods on the ground. Sketch uses survival and we make it through for two more hours. Flies and beetles are loud as things are rotting around us. We see a campsite with a ring of smoke keeping bugs at bay. We see figures sitting around. Jack calls out and then walks into the smoke. The figures are dead but sitting upright. Tenebracanthos roots around through the tents in the campsite, finds new bedrolls and deep gouges and tunnels by the smoke. Tenebracanthos also finds a healer's kit, four greater healing potions, four silver bracelets. The smoke is coming from partially stopped bottle. Sketch finds a field journal and reads that these four botanists came out to study the blight. They note that it's not natural, it's demonic, it has a singular will. They observed rotting creatures are still alive and stronger further in. Tenebracanthos pokes the tunnel and a pincer bites the end of the stick. Tenebracanthos, being the curious tree that he is, pokes it again and the ground rumbles under us and we fight.

We end up killing one of the fungus-infested antlions and three go back underground. We find out that the bottle is an ever-smoking bottle. We use it to get away from the nest of the bugs and navigate the forest some more. Tenebracanthos uses athletics to find good terrain. We see a mossy hollow with trees and a corpse covered with fungus. The carving above the corpse is a stag. Tenebracanthos investigates as mold blooms more. The same voice asks if he cannot see the beauty in the rot. Tenebracanthos in the stag converse. We notice that things are even thicker up ahead. We see a vision of an elven woman with us eating the rotting stag. "All embrace the rot at the end." We're in the festering wilds now. In the middle of the path is a doll. That stuffed doll head on a stuffed stag says "Tee-hee." Jack spooked throws his scimitar.
